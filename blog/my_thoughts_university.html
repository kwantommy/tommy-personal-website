My University Journey

by Tommy Kwan

For 4 years, I was enrolled in biomedical & electrical engineering at McMaster University in Hamilton, Ontario. University was a stressful time full of late nights and black coffees. I remember, for weeks on end, that I would leave my house at 8 in the morning and go home past midnight. The seemingly endless coursework, assignments, and projects, and When I look back to all the courses I took throughout the years, I realize that I could hardly remember some course titles let alone the course contents!

Some of my favourite courses in my undergraduate degree include modelling of biological systems, digital signal processing, data structures and algorithms, programming, microprocessor systems, and logic design.

The friends you make in university are special; after all, they are the ones who you spent countless hours complaining to about courses.

The biggest takeaway from my university career is learning how to learn. There's absolutely no way (well, except for Some geniuses that I know), to be able to learn everything in every course. How to connect dots, how to make friends, laerning to bounce back.
